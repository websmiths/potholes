Why this matters

Fox Road has degraded to the point where everyday driving is genuinely unsafe. What was once a normal residential street is now a slalom of potholes — some deep enough to swallow a wheel, several metres long, and clustered so densely that drivers cannot avoid them all.

Vehicle damage

Pothole strikes routinely cause buckled rims, blown tyres, bent suspension components and cracked alloy wheels. A single severe hit can run into thousands of dollars in repairs — costs residents are bearing privately while the road remains unrepaired.

Safety hazard

Drivers swerve across lanes to dodge holes, putting cyclists, pedestrians and oncoming traffic at risk. At night and after rain the holes are nearly invisible. Motorcyclists are especially exposed: a missed pothole can mean a serious crash.

Traffic & access

Vehicles slow to walking pace through the worst stretches. Emergency services, waste collection, delivery vans and school buses all share this road. Continued deterioration risks long-term closure or weight restrictions.

Patch-and-fail cycle

Past repairs have been shallow asphalt patches that fail within weeks. This site exists to document each cycle of failure so the pattern is visible and the case for proper resurfacing becomes undeniable.
